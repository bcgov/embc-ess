Electronic Access Agreement

This Agreement provides access to the Emergency Support Services Evacuee Registration and Assistance (ERA) tool in support of the delivery of Emergency Support Services (ESS) in British Columbia. By using ERA you signify your acceptance of this agreement and acknowledge you have read and understood the following terms.

ERA users must read and comply with the following terms to the fullest extent possible. Non-compliance will be investigated on a case-by-case basis; individuals found to be in non-compliance are subject to disciplinary action, and/or other legal remedies, pursuant to the PSLV Code of Conduct.

  1. As a condition of access to ERA, the user agrees to:
    • When access to ERA is no longer required, inform the local authority or First Nation that provided him/her access;
    • Not permit any person to use his/her BCeID and/or ERA username and password; to divulge, share or compromise his/her password; or use any other user’s BCeID or ERA username and password;
    • Not make unauthorized copies of proprietary software; reveal details of any checking, editing, validating, or security mechanisms, included in hardware or software, to any unauthorized persons; or take any action, which might reasonably be construed, as injurious or detrimental to the interests of any other user or evacuees; and
    • Undergo relevant training to support the provision of ESS and the safe and responsible useage of ERA including ESS Training provided by Emergency Management BC (EMBC).
  2. The user is responsible for all actions performed by their “BCeID" except for fraudulent use of the “BCeID” by an unauthorized third party.
  3. The user is required to adhere to all policies, standards or procedures pertaining to data security, naming conventions and good data processing practices, issued by EMBC.
  4. The user should be aware that it is possible that security can be breached through causes beyond the reasonable control of EMBC and the community. Users are urged to take full advantage of security mechanisms built into the systems and to change their password frequently.
  5. The user recognizes that to monitor security, the Ministry of Attorney General, Information Services Branch may be required to examine access by clients or users without prior notice.
  6. The user acknowledges that all data stored on the system are government records which can be accessed by Ministry officials in accordance with established policies and form a “record” under the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (see General Management Operating Policy Chapter 3.5.13 and BC Archives and Record Services, Records Information Management manual Policy No. 5.13/01).

Privacy:

  1. The user understands that personal information means recorded information about an identifiable individual other than business contact information. The identifiable individual may be an EMBC employee, a volunteer, or an evacuee. If the personal information is de-identified using an indirect identifier such as case and file number or username, the information is still considered personal information. Indirect Identifiers may identify an individual when they are connected with other pieces of information to single out an individual (e.g. age, gender, date of visit). While indirect identifiers on their own may not be personal information, they are considered personal information if they can be combined together to identify an individual. This is commonly referred to as the mosaic effect - when the combinations of data elements that in isolation look relatively innocuous, can amount to personal information.
  2. If the user is collecting personal information from an evacuee, the user must at the time of collection,
    • inform the individual of the purposes for collecting personal information;
    • limit the amount and type of personal information collected to what is necessary to fulfill the purpose;
    • make reasonable efforts to ensure that the personal information collected is accurate and complete; and
    • must only use or disclose the personal information for the purpose identified when it was collected.
  3. The user must safeguard personal information from unauthorized access, collection, use, disclosure, copying, modification or disposal by individuals from within and outside your organization.
